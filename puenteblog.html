Volunteering for Puente

Puente which stands for latter in Spanish is a program at many community colleges across California that help college students mainly children of immigrants and mostly Latino get out of community college and transfer in two years. You take two English classes with your Puente class and that same class also get to go on a trip to one university each year and each student gets appointed a mentor that they can build a relationship with. I was fortunate to be chosen to be a part of this program and I do not regret it one bit. Here we had fundraisers to raise money to but coats for kids and to but food for food banks in the winter. We also raised money for scholarships and to help any students in the program cover an emergency cost they could not afford. We also volunteered our time to go and talk to high school students who needed some motivation about going to college. Here I was involved in everything and I hope I was able to make a difference one some people lives, and I hope to become a mentor for someone in the future.
